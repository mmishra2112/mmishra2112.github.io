InvestED" application with the open university of west africa

The InvestED app allows students in refugee camps, rural villages and any place without internet connectivity to access online courseware using a 2g connection. This app automatically transmits data to and from the Open University of West Africa and the students to enable them to get micro-degrees and qualify for buisness and farm loans. Check it our here

Synke.io

Synke.io is the first product from Incendium.io, a software company built to make life easier using data and common software like google calendars. Synke.io allows companys and users to organize meetings automatically by using a data-driven algorithm to figure out when each individual is availible. I recently met with representatives from google to consider a partnership. Find out more here.

Peerlift

I am the Director of Technology and a board member at Peerlift, a non-profit organization dedicated to connecting lower and middle income students to opportunities. I created the website, algorith, server, and front-end of the website built to enable highschool students to achieve greater heights. In addition, I am collecting general data and using nearly 25 different metrics to create a sophisticated algorithm so users can see the content most relevant to them first. Check it our here peerlift.org

Club Code

Club Code is a non-profit organization that focuses on providing middle school aged students with an opportunity to learn to code in a familiar and hands on manner through creating their own video games. This is the website for more information. I started Club Code with the intention to empower youths, and have them take ownership of their learning.

Garduino

I developed, tested and built an automatic and intelligent watering system to enable the growth of my High-School's gardens. It takes in the moisture content of the garden and accordingly waters the plants. Currently I am desinging prototypes to allow the system to track weather patterns and preemptively manage the watering system.
